📌 Key Takeaways — Survey Overview

  • 383 households across 15 villages in Mulbagal taluk were surveyed
  • Avani is the largest surveyed village with 83 households
  • Only 36% of households own agricultural land
  • Ragi (finger millet) is the dominant Kharif crop (141 HHs)
  • 87% have internet access, mostly via mobile data
  • AI/IoT awareness is critically low at just 3.7% of households
  • 56 households earn from dairy, averaging ₹1.45 lakh/year
  • 28% use social media to obtain agricultural information

📱 Digital Adoption Insights

  • 343 of 383 households (89%) have internet access
  • Mobile data is overwhelmingly the dominant internet type (333 HHs)
  • Only 10 households have broadband — rural connectivity gap remains
  • 105 households (27%) use social media for agricultural information
  • AI/IoT awareness is critically low: only 14 of 363 households (3.7%)
  • Despite high mobile penetration, smart farming technology uptake lags
  • Outreach and training programs are the primary bottleneck, not device access
  • WhatsApp & YouTube are the primary social media platforms for agri info
